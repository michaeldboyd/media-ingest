# Tag Taxonomy & Guidelines

This document defines the tagging vocabulary and conventions for the media ingest skill.
Tags are the primary mechanism for searching and discovering assets in the catalog,
so richness and consistency matter.

## Core Principles

1. **Be generous** — More tags = better searchability. Aim for 10-30 per asset.
2. **Multi-level specificity** — Always include both general and specific tags.
   A golden retriever should get: `animal`, `dog`, `golden-retriever`.
3. **Lowercase, hyphenated** — All tags are lowercase. Multi-word tags use hyphens: `golden-hour`.
4. **Observable, not interpretive** — Tag what you can see, not what you assume.
5. **Consistent vocabulary** — Use the canonical tag forms listed below.

## Tag Categories

### Subject Matter
People, objects, animals, structures, vehicles, nature elements.

| Category | Example Tags |
|----------|-------------|
| People | `person`, `people`, `crowd`, `child`, `elderly`, `silhouette` |
| Animals | `animal`, `dog`, `cat`, `bird`, `wildlife`, `insect` |
| Vehicles | `car`, `truck`, `bicycle`, `boat`, `airplane`, `motorcycle` |
| Nature | `tree`, `flower`, `mountain`, `river`, `ocean`, `forest`, `desert` |
| Architecture | `building`, `house`, `bridge`, `skyscraper`, `ruin`, `church` |
| Food/Drink | `food`, `meal`, `coffee`, `restaurant`, `cooking`, `kitchen` |

## Tagging Workflow

When analyzing an image or keyframe sequence, work through these categories in order:

1. **What** is the primary subject? (Subject Matter)
2. **Where** is this? (Environment/Setting)
3. **When** — what time of day, what lighting? (Lighting & Time)
4. **How** — what's the weather/atmosphere? (Weather)
5. **Shot** — what type of shot, camera angle, movement? (Camera & Shot Type)
6. **Action** — what's happening? (Action & Activity)
7. **Feel** — what mood does this convey? (Mood & Tone)
8. **Color** — are there distinctive color characteristics? (Color Palette)
9. **Context** — what kind of production is this? (Production Context)

For video clips (multiple keyframes), consider:
- Tags that apply to the overall clip vs. specific moments
- Camera movement that spans multiple frames
- Changes in subject or action across the clip
- The narrative arc (if any) of the clip
